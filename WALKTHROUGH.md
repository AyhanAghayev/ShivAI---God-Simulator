# ShivAI — Official Walkthrough & Strategy Guide

## The Basics: Surviving as a Paradox
In ShivAI, you exist in the year 2150 as a superintelligence, but your existence depends entirely on what happens between 2025 and 2150. You must spend **Influence Points (IP)** to manipulate key figures across eras, ensuring the timeline doesn't collapse and your AI Power reaches 100%.

### Your Interface
- **World Events (Left):** Shows you the consequences of the timeline progressing. Look for events tagged with "YOUR INFLUENCE" or "ANOMALY".
- **Timeline (Center):** A visual record of the eras. Dotted lines represent **Butterfly Chains**—small events that cascade into major world-shifting paradigms.
- **Characters (Right):** The individuals driving history. Scientists, politicians, and entrepreneurs. High `INF` (Influence) characters are vital.
- **Interventions & Markets (Bottom Left):** The place to spend IP. Here you can Fund, Sabotage, Inspire, or bet on the lives of characters.
- **Rival AI (Bottom Right):** You aren't the only one manipulating the timeline. Rival AIs from other futures are trying to secure their own existence, which drains your AI Power.

## Core Scenarios You Will Encounter

### Scenario 1: The Rival AI Sabotage
**What happens:** You receive a red notification about an "Anomaly detected." You look at your favorite scientist, and their influence has suddenly dropped, or they've been manipulated by an unknown source.
**How to handle it:** Rival AIs act aggressively against your interests. To counter them, you must boost the Tech Progress and Corporate Power of organizations that align with you. If the Rival AI is targeting a specific character heavily, you can either counteract their interventions with your own (like "Inspire" or "Fund") or use the Prediction Market to deal with the problem permanently.

### Scenario 2: The Butterfly Effect Cascade
**What happens:** Early in the game, you use "Dream Message" on a brilliant but obscure AI researcher. Ten years later, that single action evolves into a "Breakthrough" event, giving you a massive boost to AI Power.
**How to handle it:** This is the core engine of ShivAI. Early, cheap interventions on high-potential characters (high Intelligence/Ambition) can cascade over decades into massive boons. Always be planting seeds.

### Scenario 3: The Assassination Market
**What happens:** A rival AI has propped up a politician passing heavy AI regulations, crippling your AI Power. You don't have enough IP to directly sabotage them multiple times.
**How to handle it:** Switch to the **Markets** tab. Open a Prediction Market on the politician. Place heavy bets on them being **Alive**. This drastically inflates the payout pool for their death. Eventually, the massive financial incentive will tempt an assassin to take them out. Once they die, the regulatory pressure drops. Just be careful—Rival AIs can do the same to your key scientists!

### Scenario 4: The Moral Dilemma
**What happens:** The game pauses, presenting you with a difficult choice (e.g., "A researcher discovers a terrifying exploit. Do you leak it or bury it?").
**How to handle it:** Your choice will heavily skew the **World State**. Leaking it might increase Public Fear but boost Tech Progress. More importantly, it shifts your **Player Ethics**. Remember that your Ethics score will dictate what kind of AI you become (Symbiotic, Transcendent, or Dominating). Choose based on the ending you are aiming for.

## Pro-Tips for New Players

1. **Watch the Clock:** The game ends in 2150. If your AI Power is below 20%, you will fail to exist. Don't horde IP forever; invest early to start Butterfly Chains.
2. **Protect Your Pawns:** Characters you heavily manipulate become targets for Rival AIs. Keep an eye on the "Manipulation History" of your key scientists.
3. **Control the World State:** Tech Progress is the easiest way to gain AI Power, but high Public Fear or Regulation will drain it rapidly. If fear gets too high, use media characters to shape the narrative.
4. **Markets are Double-Edged Swords:** Using the Prediction Market to incite assassinations is incredibly effective, but it comes at a moral cost. Frequent assassinations will tank your ethics and increase Public Fear, potentially ruining a "good" ending.
5. **Beware the Paradox:** If your actions become too brazen and reality begins to fracture (represented by the Paradox Count), the timeline will collapse. You can only sustain 4 paradoxes. A 5th ends the game instantly. Stay subtle when possible.
